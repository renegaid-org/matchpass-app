# Legitimate Interest Assessment — [Club Name]

## Processing Activity
MatchPass credential scanning, card issuance, and cross-club ban propagation for matchday safety.

## Legitimate Interest
Safety of persons attending sporting events at [Club Name] ground. This is the same legal basis used by venues, festivals, and transport operators for safety-related data processing (GDPR Article 6(1)(f)).

## Necessity
- Credential scanning is necessary to verify that an attendee is not currently banned or suspended.
- Photo display is necessary for visual identity matching at the gate (preventing credential sharing).
- Card issuance is necessary to record antisocial behaviour for graduated sanctions.
- Cross-club propagation is necessary because fans attend multiple grounds and a ban at one club must be enforceable at others.

## Balancing Test
- Data is minimised: no names stored in matchpass.app, pseudonymous pubkeys only, photos fetched and discarded.
- Scan logs retained for 30 days only (covers challenge window), then purged.
- Fans are notified when scanned (via Signet notification). Fans can challenge any card.
- The safety interest (thousands of people in a confined space) outweighs the minimal privacy intrusion of a 2-second photo flash from a photo the fan uploaded themselves.

## Conclusion
Processing is proportionate and necessary for the legitimate interest of matchday safety.
