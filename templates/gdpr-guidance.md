# MatchPass GDPR Guidance for Clubs

## Plain-English Summary

MatchPass is a digital tool that replaces paper-based banning lists and manual identity checks at the gate. It processes less personal data than most existing systems (no names, no addresses — just a pseudonymous identifier and a photo flash).

## What Your Solicitor Needs to Know

1. **Legal basis:** Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)) — safety of persons at sporting events.
2. **Data controller:** Your club. Not MatchPass. You own the data and the compliance.
3. **What's stored:** Pseudonymous fan identifiers (Signet pubkeys), card records, scan logs. No names.
4. **What's NOT stored:** Fan photos (fetched from Blossom and discarded). Fan names. Fan addresses.
5. **Retention:** Scan logs 30 days. Cards until expired. Bans until expired. Season-end purge.
6. **Cross-club sharing:** Ban records (pubkey + reason + expiry) shared with other pilot clubs. Controller-controller agreement template provided.
7. **Fan rights:** Challenge process for all cards. Subject access requests via club contact. Deletion requests honoured subject to active safety obligations.

## Recommendation
Have your solicitor review the LIA and privacy notice before the first match using MatchPass. This should be a short review — the templates are specific and the data processing is minimal.
